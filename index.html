A snapshot of pre-college life

    The Earlier Days

    Discovering a persistent interest in writing and creating

  • An elementary school assignment sparked my interest in fiction writing.

  • It, like many of my early stories, were teen detective stories that featured a loyal golden retriever sidekick.

  • I took off with writing more tales, including one on the Bermuda cave and one on teens with superpowers

    High School

    Taking a more scientific approach to examining people and their lives

  • For my AP Spanish class, I wrote a poem in Pablo Neruda's "Ode to" format.

  • "Oda a los ojos" was published in our chapter's Spanish Honor Society magazine.

  • One lazy summer day in the break before senior year, I decided to look around our house's basement.

  • I dug up my uncle's college psychology textbook and started reading...








    • On to college

University of Chicago

"Life of the Mind"

    Freshman Year

    Exploring my interest in clinical psychology and neuroscience

  • I came in with the intent to major in Psychology and Biology (Neuroscience).

  • I wanted to study processes and pathways. And the brain and the mind were what interested me.

  • I was drawn to clinical work, so over the summer I joined the Adult Weight and Eating Disorders Lab.

  • I created online verisons of psychiatric questionnaires in SurveyGizmo and SurveyMonkey.

  • I also wrote SPSS syntax to clean the output psychiatric survey datasets.

  • It was my first painful but critical introduction to code.

    Sophomore Year

    Deeper diving into creative writing and clinical research.

  • I took a creative writing class on crime fiction and started writing again for the first time in a long while.

  • I explored dialogue and complicated relationships with Rina and Jakko.

  • Over the summer, I took Organic Chemistry.

  • It was a rough time for me: I slept 4-6 hours nightly, working throughout the day with no breaks.

  • I was hitting right up to my limits, but I persisted. I discovered that anything can be made possible--what matters is whether the time and effort is worth it in the end.

  • From spring through the summer I worked as a Marketing and Sales Assistant at the Chicago Electrical Trauma Research Institute and Avocet

  • To help update both companies' websites, I learned basic HTML and CSS

  • I also edited and formatted the content of marketing brochures and emails

    Junior Year

    My first introduction to peer critiques and living in a new country

  • I took an Intermediate Fiction Workshop

  • This was where I received and gave peer critiques for the first time

  • In the spring, I studied abroad in Barcelona

  • I read, wrote papers and listened to lectures entirely in Spanish.

    Senior Year

    Learning object oriented programming

  • I took an intro programming course, which taught me Java and Python

  • This class took up >10 hours/week for me.
    For projects, I modeled a simple betting strategy and mimicked a stock exchange transaction among others



    • To my first full-time job

Massachusetts General Hospital

Life in Boston

    Year 1

    Becoming a full-time clinical researcher

  • I wanted exposure to patient interaction to help me decide between a clinical psychology or neuroscience research PhD

  • I coordinated patients’ appointments and ran the TMS clinic, performing brain stimulation as the technician.

  • For research experience, I also guided subjects through lengthy studies that included interviews, computer tasks, brain imaging and brain stimulation.

  • But in the end,
    I wasn't in love with my job.







    Year 2

    Transitioning into a programmer, data analyst and ultimately into UX

  • I started looking at other career paths, from digital forensics to data science and I discovered UX.

  • I started reading more, from books by Steve Krug to signing up for weekly UX articles.

  • I attended talks, conferences and networking events sponsored by UXPA and General Assembly.

  • I signed up for MIT's 6.148 Web Programming lectures which I attended for 2 weeks, taking classes by day and working by night.

  • Within the lab, I switched gears to do more mentally stimulating work.

  • I programmed
    psych stimuli experiments in E-prime and Presentation, brushing up on my object oriented coding skills.

  • I cleaned and analyzed data with STATA syntax, incorporating programming techniques I had learned to expedite the process.

  • I wrote a review paper on TMS for treatment-resistant OCD patients.

  • I also took over broader organizational tasks

  • I coordinated our departmental transition to EPIC and adding a new clinician and exam room to our schedule.

  • I was suggested by several people I worked with for the Program Manager position, but I wanted something different from admin.

  • I did front end development for the 2016 Angular Attack Hackathon
